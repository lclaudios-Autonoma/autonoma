AutoNoma is a B2C asset-light SaaS model targeting women with high mental load, limited time, and a need for continuous support in daily decision-making and task resolution.

Core thesis: capture a large free user base, convert a portion into affordable paid plans, and scale with high LTV and controlled churn — operating lean with variable AI costs and a compact team.

02

Problem & Opportunity

  • The target audience faces an overload of demands, fragmented decision-making, difficulty prioritizing, and the lack of a single trusted point of support for everyday matters.
  • The opportunity lies in offering an assistant that doesn't just answer questions, but reduces mental load, executes actions, and maintains context across interactions — functioning as a companion, not a tool.
  • The addressable market benefits from low entry friction: a free plan for acquisition, affordable paid plans for conversion, and expansion potential across LATAM in subsequent phases.

Key Risks

  • Variable AI cost: monitor cost per interaction, caching, usage limits, and margin per plan — any API price increase directly impacts unit margin.
  • Conversion and churn: validate with real cohorts whether projected conversion and churn rates hold — these are the model's primary sensitivity drivers.
  • Pricing: test willingness to pay; with Essencial at R$ 39.90 and Autônoma at R$ 59.90, the ~50% jump needs to be justified by clear perceived value.
  • Output consistency: there are discrepancies between some numbers in the Monthly Base and Scenario Summary that should be reconciled before formal presentation.
  • Privacy and trust: since the product handles memory and personal topics — security, consent, and data policies are critical elements for retention and regulatory compliance.
